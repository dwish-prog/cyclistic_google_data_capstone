# Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate

Capstone project: Cyclistic bikeshare

Using historical trip data provided publicly by Divvy/Chicago Dept. of Transportation.
Created analysis presentation for fictional comapny 'Cyclistic'
Business problem: Understand behavior based on rider type in order to convert casual riders into more profitable annual memberships.

Data source: https://divvy-tripdata.s3.amazonaws.com/index.html
Data License agreement: https://www.divvybikes.com/data-license-agreement

Combined the last 12 months of trip data (Jul 25 - Jun 26) into a single dataset containing c. 5.9 million rows.

Tools: MS SQL, Excel, Powerpoint

Key insights:
- Casual riders especially use the service during the summer months.
- Annual members tend to use the service more during the week, casual rider trips then surge at the weekend.
- Casual riders account for most of the long (30+ minute) trips, annual members take shorter rides.
- There are clear ‘commuter spikes’ during midweek rush hours for annual members. Casual rider trips follow a softer ‘afternoon and weekend leisure’ curve.
- Annual members tend to start downtown in commercial areas, casual riders nearer the waterfront park and recreation areas.

Business recommendations:
- Target marketing during spring as casual riders begin using the service more.
- Design targeted, limited-time promotions that frame the annual membership as a cost-saving tool for high-frequency weekend and seasonal leisure users.
- Target marketing around recreational areas of the city.
